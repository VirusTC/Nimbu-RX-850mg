### A. Counteracting Urinary Relative Supersaturation ($\text{SS}$)
Nimbu-RX-850mg targets the core mathematical vector driving mineral stone diseases (Calcium Oxalate/Phosphate Nephrolithiasis). The plant-based citrate ions competitively chelate free ionized calcium ($\text{Ca}^{2+}$) based on its thermodynamic stability constant ($K_f = 10^{4.85}$):

$$a_{\text{Ca}^{2+}} = \frac{[[\text{Ca}\cdot\text{Cit}]^{-}]}{K_f \times a_{\text{Cit}^{3-}}}$$

By maximizing the available urinary citrate pool ($a_{\text{Cit}^{3-}}$), the free calcium ion activity ($a_{\text{Ca}^{2+}}$) collapses, driving the relative supersaturation ratio below the nucleation threshold ($\text{SS} < 1.0$), forcing the dynamic dissolution of crystal matrices.

### B. Systemic Bicarbonate Generation
Once absorbed through the gastrointestinal tract, the organic citric acid undergoes complete physical oxidation within hepatic tissue pathways to produce twelve equivalents of vital alkaline bicarbonate buffer:

$$2\text{C}_6\text{H}_8\text{O}_7 + 18\text{O}_2 \longrightarrow 12\text{HCO}_3^- + 12\text{H}^+ + 6\text{H}_2\text{O}$$

This metabolic conversion shifts urinary pH balances upward into an optimal therapeutic window ($6.2 - 6.8$), maintaining systemic acid-base homeostasis without introducing synthetic mineral stresses.
