**Analytical System Tracker:** Remote Patient Portal Outcomes  
**Statistical Target:** Connecting Home Audio Monitoring Data to Treatment Safety Profiles

## 1. Statistical Modeling of Acoustic Distress Signals
To evaluate if remote audio alerts correlate with actual patient intolerance to the acid matrix, telehealth teams use a standard logistic regression model:

$$\ln\left(\frac{P(\text{Intolerance})}{1 - P(\text{Intolerance})}\right) = \beta_0 + \beta_1(\Delta SPL) + \beta_2(T_{\text{frequency}}) + \epsilon$$

Where:
* $P(\text{Intolerance})$ = The probability that a patient must pause therapy due to side effects like gastric pyrosis or hidden mouth sores.
* $\Delta SPL$ = The maximum decibel increase captured by the room monitor during or right after a dose.
* $T_{\text{frequency}}$ = The specific audio frequency duration matching coughing or choking profiles.
* $\beta_1$ and $\beta_2$ = Regression coefficients showing how strongly acoustic spikes predict clinical intolerance.

## 2. Purity & Enamel Wear Variance Tracking
Because this flagship product uses a plant-based, unadulterated process without added synthetic mineral salts, it avoids placing a mineral overload strain on the kidneys. However, it still maintains an initial local acidity before it is metabolized. 

We track the relationship between patient water-rinse compliance and dental sensitivity trends over a 90-day window using a standard analysis of variance (ANOVA) equation:

$$F = \frac{\text{Variance Between Patient Groups (Rinse vs. Non-Rinse)}}{\text{Variance Within Patient Groups}}$$

A statistically significant result ($F > F_{\text{critical}}, \ p \leq 0.05$) confirms that enforcing the 5-minute water rinse step via the remote room camera preserves dental enamel health while delivering full systemic alkalization benefits.
