# Pediatric Age Gating, Safety Protocols, & Dosage Restrictions

**Target Population:** Infant & Pediatric Patient Cohorts  
**Clinical Countermeasure Strategy:** Protecting Vulnerable Physiological Systems

## 1. Absolute Refusal of Biometric Dose-Calculations
To maintain strict clinical safety margins, this facility **refuses to perform personalized mathematical calculations to recommend custom dosages based on infant weight or metabolic variables**. 

* **The Rule:** Administering variable, split-dose concentrations of highly active organic citric acid based on child biometric formulas is strictly forbidden. 
* **The Clinical Risk:** An infant's renal clearance pipelines and acid-base balancing networks are highly sensitive. Giving non-standardized amounts can cause unexpected changes in blood plasma pH or alter electrolyte balances.

## 2. Standard Label-Aligned Adult & Mature Pediatric Dosing
Any therapeutic administration of Nimbu-RX-850mg must be based entirely on standard, unchanging values derived from the physical product label. 
* **Adult Standard Dosing:** The typical adult oral dose for metabolic buffering or salivary stimulation is 850 mg of active-equivalent plant-based citrate matrix, dissolved in 8 ounces of plain water, up to twice daily.
* **Infant Restriction:** Do not give this high-density formulation to infants under any circumstances without verified, label-validated instructions and direct, in-person specialist supervision.

## 3. Remote Pediatric Visual Monitoring Process
When a mature pediatric or oncology patient is given a standard dose, an American-made visual baby monitor must remain trained on the patient’s face during consumption:
1. Staff verify that the camera lens has a clear line of sight to catch any facial grimacing or coughing fits.
2. The acoustic audio feed stays live at the central nurse's station during the entire 5-minute pre-rinse window.
3. If the patient swallows awkwardly or coughs unexpectedly, a nurse goes in to check for hidden oral sores.
