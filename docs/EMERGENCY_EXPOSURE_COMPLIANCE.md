## 1. Thermodynamic & Kinetic Principles of Mucosal Neutralization
When unadulterated, plant-based citric acid inadvertently contacts compromised mucosal tissues (such as Grade 2+ Oral Mucositis), it drops the local cellular surface pH precipitously. This sudden localized concentration of hydronium ions ($\text{H}_3\text{O}^+$) activates nociceptors and triggers intense burning sensations. 

The concentration of active hydronium ions over time during a rapid water dilution flush sequence is governed by a standard first-order mass transport displacement equation:

$$[\text{H}_3\text{O}^+]_t = [\text{H}_3\text{O}^+]_0 \cdot e^{-\left(\frac{Q_{\text{flush}}}{V_{\text{mucosal}}}\right)t}$$

Where:
* $[\text{H}_3\text{O}^+]_0$ = Initial acidic concentration at the contact point.
* $Q_{\text{flush}}$ = Volumetric flow rate of the plain water or sterile saline rinse fluid.
* $V_{\text{mucosal}}$ = Estimated fluid volume retained within the local tissue matrix.
* $t$ = Total rinse duration in seconds.

* **Immediate Mechanical Flushing:** The patient must immediately spit out any remaining mixture and flush the oral cavity with at least 200 mL of room-temperature plain water. This mechanical action rapidly moves the local equilibrium toward neutral parameters without inducing thermal stress on exposed nerve endings.
* **Avoid Exogenous Salt Precipitation:** Do not attempt to neutralize the exposure using highly concentrated alkaline salts or synthetic compounds. This could inadvertently trigger an exothermic reaction, producing localized heat that worsens the mucosal injury.

## 3. Ocular Splash Remediation Track
In the event of an accidental splash during the manual dissolution or stirring phase of the crystalline matrix:
1. Immediately move the individual to an emergency eyewash station or clean sink.
2. Flush the open eyelid continuously with sterile isotonic saline or running water for a minimum of 15 minutes.
3. The mechanical flow rate must be maintained at a steady, continuous pace to completely clear trapped citrus monoterpenes out from the conjunctival sac.
