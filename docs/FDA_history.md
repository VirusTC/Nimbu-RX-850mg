I. The FDA Regulatory History of Citric Acid
--------------------------------------------

The administrative timeline of citric acid moves through three distinct phases: initial historical adoption, statutory affirmation, and modern purity standardizations. [1, 2, 3]

1\. The Pre-FDA Era & GRAS Grandfathering (Pre-1958)
----------------------------------------------------

Citric acid was initially isolated from raw lemon juice in 1784 by Carl Scheele using a calcium precipitation method. In 1919, industrial-scale mycological fermentation via *Aspergillus niger* was commercialized. Because manufactured citric acid was already widely distributed across the global food supply before the passage of the 1958 Food Additives Amendment, the FDA classified it as Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) under [21 CFR Part 182](https://archive.epa.gov/pesticides/reregistration/web/pdf/citric_acid.pdf). [2, 3, 4, 5]

2\. Statutory Affirmation & Direct Ingredient Listing (1994)
------------------------------------------------------------

Following comprehensive safety evaluations, the FDA published a Final Rule in the Federal Register ([59 FR 63894](https://www.govinfo.gov/app/details/FR-1994-12-12/94-30496)) affirming the GRAS status of citric acid and its associated calcium, potassium, and sodium salts as direct human food ingredients. This codified citric acid under [21 CFR 184.1033](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-B/part-184/subpart-B/section-184.1033), permitting its use with no limitations other than Current Good Manufacturing Practices (cGMP). [1, 5, 6, 7]

3\. Modern USP/NF Monograph Requirements
----------------------------------------

For pharmaceutical applications, the FDA mandates compliance with the United States Pharmacopeia (USP) monograph. The standard requires an assay composition of $99.5\%$ to $100.5\%$ anhydrous $\text{C}_6\text{H}_8\text{O}_7$ and enforces strict thresholds on dangerous process byproducts: [8, 9, 10]

-   Oxalic Acid Limit: $\leq 0.036\%$
-   Residue on Ignition: $\leq 0.05\%$
-   Bacterial Endotoxins: Regulated under USP $\langle 85 \rangle$ for injectable or dialysis formulations. [9, 10, 11]
