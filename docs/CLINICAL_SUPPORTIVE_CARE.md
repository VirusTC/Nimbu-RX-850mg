# Clinical Application Profile for Oncology Supportive Care

**Target Indication:** Radiation-Induced Oral Xerostomia & Dysgeusia  
**Reference Model:** National Cancer Institute (NCI) Clinical Trial Design

## 1. Mechanisms of Supportive Care Action
Nimbu-RX-850mg acts as a non-invasive, plant-based therapeutic intervention for individuals undergoing oncology regimens:

* **Salivary Induction via Organic Acid:** The concentrated organic citric acid interacts directly with mucosal receptors, stimulating the autonomic pathways of the submandibular and parotid glands to promote natural saliva secretion. This counteracts treatment-related xerostomia.
* **Dysgeusia Modulation via Native Volatiles:** Farmed lime oils yield high levels of natural D-limonene. This volatile compound acts on modified taste profiles to mask or bypass the persistent metallic taste induced by cisplatin or other standard chemotherapeutic agents.

## 2. Mass Balance and Target Clearance Curves
The systemic behavior of the plant-based citrate pool follows a one-compartmental biological tracking sequence:

$$\text{AUC}_{0\rightarrow\infty} = \frac{F \times \text{Dose}}{\text{Cl}}$$

Assuming a plant-based bioavailability coefficient ($F \approx 0.85$) preserved via our whole-food freeze-drying process, an 850 mg administered dose establishes an extended systemic presence profile compared to isolated alternatives:

$$\text{AUC} = \frac{0.85 \times 850\text{ mg}}{5.25\text{ L/h}} \approx 137.62\text{ mg}\cdot\text{h/L}$$

## 3. Mandatory Safety Frameworks
* **Mucositis Screening:** Administration must be suspended immediately if the individual presents with Active Grade 2 or higher Oral Mucositis. The natural acidity of the matrix will exacerbate active ulcerations.
* **Enamel Protection Protocols:** Due to the low pH of organic citrus extracts, individuals must perform a plain water rinse five minutes post-consumption to clear residual surface acids from enamel pathways.
