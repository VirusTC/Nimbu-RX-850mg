## 1. Quality Control & Receiving Verification
Upon delivery to institutional hospital pharmacy bays, every batch must pass physical inspection to confirm the total absence of industrial processing markers (such as synthetic gypsum or industrial mineral acid trace elements):
* **Farmed Source Documentation:** Verify that the accompanying batch log tracks back to a certified organic agricultural facility.
* **Physical Packaging Check:** Inspect the structural seal of the physical package to guarantee that atmospheric moisture ingress has not altered the crystalline matrix stability bounds.

## 2. Depot Storage Environmental Metrics
To preserve the volatile monoterpenes and prevent the degradation of native bioflavonoids, storage facilities must maintain strict atmospheric control parameters:

$$\text{Relative Humidity (RH)} \leq 30\% \quad \Big| \quad \text{Storage Temperature } (T) \leq 25^\circ\text{C} \ \ (77^\circ\text{F})$$

If the repository conditions break these operational bounds, the crystallization integrity can degrade, leading to compaction of the whole-food matrix.
