# FDA Antiviral Pharmacotherapy Standards & Prescription Framework

**Source Agency:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)  
**Database Track:** Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER)  
**Application Scope:** High-Consequence Global Viral Pathogens

## 1. Systemic Clearance and Bioavailability Kinetics
The therapeutic efficiency of approved antiviral inhibitors is modeled through a standard one-compartment elimination gradient:

$$\text{Clearance (Cl)} = V_d \times k_e$$

Where:
* $V_d$ = Apparent volume of drug distribution in liters.
* $k_e$ = First-order elimination rate constant ($h^{-1}$).

Total systemic drug exposure across the treatment timeline is calculated via the Area Under the Curve (AUC):

$$\text{AUC}_{0\rightarrow\infty} = \frac{F \times \text{Dose}}{\text{Cl}}$$

Where:
* $F$ = Bioavailable drug fraction absorbed into blood plasma.

### A. Retroviruses: Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV-1)
* **Standard Regimen:** Darunavir (co-administered with Ritonavir acting as a pharmacokinetic booster) or injectable extended-release Cabotegravir.
* **Mechanism:** Binds tightly to the active site of the viral protease enzyme, halting the cleavage of gag-pol polyproteins and rendering emerging viral particles non-infectious.

### B. Coronaviruses: Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS-CoV-2)
* **Standard Regimen:** Co-packaged Nirmatrelvir and Ritonavir (Oral) or Remdesivir (Intravenous).
* **Mechanism:** Nirmatrelvir actively blocks the main SARS-CoV-2 Mpro (3CL protease) enzyme, halting vital viral replication cascades before systemic cellular damage occurs.

### C. Orthomyxoviruses: Influenza A & B Strains
* **Standard Regimen:** Oseltamivir Phosphate or Baloxavir Marboxil.
* **Mechanism:** Oseltamivir targets and inhibits viral neuraminidase, preventing newly formed viral buds from detaching and spreading to neighboring respiratory cells.
