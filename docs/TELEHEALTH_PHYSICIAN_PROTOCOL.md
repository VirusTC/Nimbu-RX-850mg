# Telehealth Clinical Consultation & Prescription Protocol

**Clinical Protocol Mapping:** Distal Renal Tubular Acidosis, Hypocitraturia, & Oncology Supportive Care  
**Assessment Modality:** Synchronous Video Telehealth Interface

## 1. Remote Pre-Screening Diagnostics
Prior to authorizing an electronic prescription for Nimbu-RX-850mg, the attending physician must verify the following remote data elements:
* **24-Hour Urinary Volume:** Confirm the patient maintains fluid hydration metrics ensuring an output of $> 2.0\text{ Liters}$ per day.
* **Intracellular pH Indicators:** Review baseline laboratory serum potassium tiers. Persistent hypokalemia indicates an intracellular acidosis path that accelerates renal proximal tubule citrate reabsorption.

## 2. In Vivo Thermodynamic Target Mapping
The physician maps the target dosage to lower the relative supersaturation profile ($\text{SS}$) of urinary minerals below the nucleation threshold:

$$\text{SS}_{\text{CaOx}} = \frac{a_{\text{Ca}^{2+}} \times a_{\text{Ox}^{2-}}}{K_{sp}} < 1.0$$

The 850 mg plant-based citric acid dose undergoes direct metabolic conversion within the liver to supply twelve equivalents of systemic bicarbonate buffer:

$$2\text{C}_6\text{H}_8\text{O}_7 + 18\text{O}_2 \longrightarrow 12\text{HCO}_3^- + 12\text{H}^+ + 6\text{H}_2\text{O}$$

This generated bicarbonate increases the urinary pH toward an optimal range ($6.2 - 6.8$), preventing solid-phase crystal precipitation.

## 3. Mandatory In-Person Screening Trigger
* **Oral Mucositis Exclusion:** Telehealth providers must visually confirm via high-resolution camera that the patient does not present with active, open chemotherapy-induced oral mucositis. 
* **Action:** If Grade 2 or higher mucosal ulcerations are observed, the protocol must be suspended immediately. The natural low pH of the citrus extract will cause immediate, severe tissue burning.
